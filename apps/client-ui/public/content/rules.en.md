# Official Rules for Wizard and Anniversary Special Cards (AMIGO) - Consolidated English Rulebook

## Executive Summary

Wizard is a trick-taking prediction game: in every round you predict exactly how many tricks you will win, then play to hit that number as precisely as possible. The core rules are simple (follow suit, trump, Wizards/Jesters), while the tactical depth comes from combining prediction with trick control.

Anniversary and special editions extend this core with special cards that intentionally disrupt either trick hierarchy (for example Dragon/Fairy) or planning stability (for example Bomb, Cloud, Juggler).

## Standard Rule Set for Wizard (60 cards)

### Components and Objective

Wizard is played with **60 cards**: four suits with values **1-13** (52 number cards total), plus **4 Wizards** and **4 Jesters**.
The objective is to score the most points over multiple rounds by making **accurate trick predictions**.

### Setup

One player starts as dealer (handled by the software here). The dealer rotates **clockwise** after each round.

### Round Structure

Each round has four phases: **deal cards**, **predict tricks**, **play tricks**, **score points**.

**Cards per round:** Round 1 deals 1 card per player, round 2 deals 2 cards, and so on.
**Number of rounds (until the deck is fully consumed):**

- 3 players: 20 rounds
- 4 players: 15 rounds
- 5 players: 12 rounds
- 6 players: 10 rounds

### Trump Determination

After dealing, the top card of the remaining stack is revealed:

- If it is a **number card**, its **suit is trump** for this round.
- If it is a **Wizard**, the dealer chooses a trump suit.
- If it is a **Jester**, there is **no trump suit** this round.
- **Final round:** In the base game, the final round is played **without trump** because all cards are dealt.

### Bidding (Prediction)

After dealing, each player predicts how many tricks they will win. The **player to the dealer's left** starts. Bids are written down and repeated before the first trick starts.

### Trick Rules (Follow Suit, Wizards, Jesters)

A trick is played with each player contributing **one card** in turn.

- The player who leads may play **any card**.
- After that, **follow suit** applies: if you have a card of the led suit, you must play that suit.
- If you cannot follow suit, you may play any other suit, trump, Wizard, or Jester.
- **Wizards and Jesters** can always be played, even if you could follow suit; they belong to **no suit**.

**Trick winner (base rule):**

1. the **first Wizard** played in the trick, otherwise
2. the **highest trump card**, otherwise
3. the **highest card of the originally led suit**

**Special case when a trick is led by Wizard or Jester:**

- Lead with **Wizard**: everyone may play any card; the first Wizard wins.
- Lead with **Jester**: everyone may play freely until a number card or Wizard appears. Once the first number card appears, that suit becomes the suit to follow for the rest of that trick. If the first non-Jester is a Wizard, free play remains.
- Jesters normally lose every trick; if only Jesters are played, the first Jester wins.

### Taking Tricks and Counting

The winner of a trick takes those cards and places them face down. That winner leads the next trick. Won tricks should be arranged so the count is always visible.

### Scoring

At round end, points are awarded based on predicted vs. actual tricks:

- **Exact prediction:** 10 points per won trick **plus** 20 bonus points.
- **Missed prediction:** -10 points per trick difference (over or under).

The game ends after the final round; highest score wins. Ties are possible.

### Official Base-Game Variant

The base rules include **Plus/Minus One** as an explicit variant: total bids may not equal the number of tricks in that round.
Important: this is a **variant**, not a mandatory default rule.

### Trick Resolution in the Base Game

Practical resolution sequence (consistent with the rules):

1. Determine the **followed suit**: this is the suit of the first number card in the trick; if the trick started with Jester, the first number card sets it.
2. Check whether at least one **Wizard** is in the trick: if yes, the **first** Wizard wins.
3. Otherwise, if trump exists: highest trump wins.
4. Otherwise: highest card of the followed suit wins.

### Shared Rule for Anniversary Special Cards

In anniversary editions, special cards (in varying counts) are shuffled into the normal deck. Standard Wizard flow still applies. Because the deck is larger, even the final round still leaves a card for trump determination (unlike the base game).

### Effect Order with Multiple Special Cards in One Trick

Effects should be resolved in a defined order, especially when Cloud, Juggler, and Witch overlap.

Consistent practical sequence (based on official examples):

1. Play the trick and declare suits for Cloud/Juggler where needed.
2. Determine trick result: who wins (or Bomb: nobody).
3. Resolve post-trick effects in this priority:
   - Juggler: pass cards (also when Bomb is present; except in final trick).
   - Cloud: adjust prediction (in 30-year edition immediately after trick; with Juggler+Cloud, do Juggler first, then Cloud).
   - Witch: exchange only after all other effects.

If your group uses the Cloud version where adjustment happens at end of round, move Cloud resolution accordingly to round end, while Juggler remains trick-bound.

For private groups this is not a mandatory global rule. In the base game, Plus/Minus One is listed as a variant.
